Congratulations on finding this unassuming corner.

I am a master's student, currently torn between "doing a PhD" and "~~getting a job~~". I have academic impulses but neither the academic ability nor the perseverance, and no passion for entering the workplace either. I am right in the middle of a drifting period.

The original impulse for building this little site was to escape my thesis: I didn't want to write it, so I busied myself with site-building instead. This site is powered by [Hexo](https://hexo.io/zh-cn/) with the [Fluid theme](https://github.com/fluid-dev/hexo-theme-fluid), with some personalization on top — my thanks to the developers involved.

Here I will post articles from time to time — sometimes polished writing, sometimes ramblings and sleep-talk.

I don't know who you are, but somehow it feels like a very lucky thing to have met you.

Thank you for coming here.

Have a nice day.

Update, 2023.7.31

After passing my dissertation proposal defense, I spent quite a while reading. Reading really is a way to make myself calm and focused. So let it be — keep it up, read more, write more!

I didn't get the CSC funding, and my dream of going abroad fell through. My overseas advisor said this might be a blessing in disguise. Not going abroad is, in a sense, the general solution — an outcome everyone can accept, my mother especially. She was overjoyed and kept saying "it's good not to go abroad" for many days.

Many classmates' lives have already moved on to the next stage, while I always seem to be on the road. The so-called "doubting Wanyu, understanding Wanyu, becoming Wanyu" is just the same thing in another form.

Staying in Shanghai can also be a beginning. This is a city I love, and here too I want to have my own driving force — to live, research, and work well.

Anyway: choose bravely, act actively, bear the consequences, keep moving forward.
