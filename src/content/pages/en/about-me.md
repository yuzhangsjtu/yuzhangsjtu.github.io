Congratulations on finding this unassuming corner.

I am a master's student, currently torn between "doing a PhD" and "~~getting a job~~" — I have academic impulses but neither academic ability nor persistence, and no passion for entering the workplace either. I am in a period of drifting.

The original impulse for building this little site was to escape my thesis: I didn't want to write it, so I fiddled with site-building instead. The site is powered by [Hexo](https://hexo.io/) with the [Fluid theme](https://github.com/fluid-dev/hexo-theme-fluid), with some personalization on top — my thanks to the developers.

Here I will publish articles from time to time — sometimes polished writing, sometimes murmurs and sleep-talk.

I don't know who you are, but I feel lucky to have met you.

Thank you for coming.

Have a nice day.

Update, 2023.7.31

After passing my dissertation proposal defense, I spent a good while reading. Reading really is a way to quiet down and concentrate. Keep it up then — read more, write more!

I didn't get the CSC funding; the dream of going abroad fell through. My overseas advisor said this might be a blessing in disguise. Not going abroad is, in a sense, the general solution — an outcome everyone can accept, my mother especially. She was overjoyed and kept saying "better not to go abroad" for days.

Many classmates' lives have moved on to the next stage, while I always seem to be on the road. The so-called arc of "doubting Wanyu, understanding Wanyu, becoming Wanyu" — only in another form.

Staying in Shanghai can also be a beginning. This is a city I love, and here too I want to find my own driving force — to live, research, and work well.

Anyway: choose bravely, act actively, bear the consequences, keep moving forward.
