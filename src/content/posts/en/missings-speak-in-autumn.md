### Longings That Speak in Autumn

<br/>

By Pan Yungui

<br/>

The story is woken by the sound of falling leaves  
This autumn, wind brushes the treetops where sunlight thins  
You walk toward the northern edge of the city, your trace vanishes  
The rain departs without farewell, each bout sadder than the last  
I see rose-hued longing, a squirming worm inside my heart  
In a corner you’d never notice  
Wanting to grow into a flying butterfly  

<br/>

The longest distance in the world is longing  
Longer than the equator, longer than latitude and longitude, longer than every light-year  
You are the castle sleeping inside longing  
Shrouded by forest, iron gate double-locked, your face blank  
I travel over mountains and forded streams to see you  
I have met swans that speak, glimpsed giraffes that glow  
But I no longer catch sight of you  

<br/>

Sometimes I recall the days we sat on the rooftop blowing wind  
Wisps of cloud and rose, the sunset like an eighteen-year-old bride  
On your back wings emerged, your body alight with snowflake glow  
You said you were an angel, and would guard certain souls for a lifetime  
I didn’t believe, shook my head  
You fluttered your wings, lifted up in an instant  
The smile at the corner of your lips became Wordsworth’s gilded sonnets  

<br/>

Now, I have come to believe  
You are the most distinctive person in this world  
Owning Kaede Rukawa’s temperature, the Mona Lisa’s smile  
I don’t know why I miss you so profoundly  
Want to write you a long, long letter  
On an autumn afternoon, the Booted Cat strides across the eaves' ridge  
I think, that is you  
You who have "finally" return to see me  
You who are about to finally say, "I like you too."
