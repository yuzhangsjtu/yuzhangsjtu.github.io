### Those Longings Speak in Autumn

<br/>

By Pan Yungui

<br/>

The story is woken by the sound of falling leaves

This autumn, the wind brushes the treetops where sunlight grows faint

You walk toward the northern edge of the city, your trace disappears

The rain that leaves without a goodbye falls more bitterly with each spell

I see that rose-colored longing is a worm wriggling deep in my heart

In a corner you cannot perceive

Wanting to become a butterfly in flight

<br/>

The longest distance in the world is longing

Longer than the equator, longer than latitude and longitude, longer than all the light-years

You are the castle that sleeps inside longing

Hidden by the forest, the iron gate tightly locked, without expression

I cross mountains and wade through waters wanting to see you

I have met swans that speak, met giraffes that glow

Yet I no longer see you

<br/>

Sometimes I recall the days we sat on the rooftop in the wind

The clouds and rosy mist drifting, the sunset like an eighteen-year-old bride

Wings grew from your back, your body studded with the light of snowflakes

You said you were an angel, that you would guard certain people for a lifetime

I didn't believe it, and shook my head

You fluttered your wings and rose into the air in an instant

The smile at the corner of your mouth became Wordsworth's golden sonnet
<br/>


Now, I have come to believe

You are the most special person in this world

With the warmth of Kaede Rukawa, with the smile of the Mona Lisa

I don't know why I miss you so much

I want to write you a long, long letter

On an autumn afternoon, Puss in Boots walks across the eaves

I think, that is you

You who have finally come back to see me

You who will finally say, "I like you too"
