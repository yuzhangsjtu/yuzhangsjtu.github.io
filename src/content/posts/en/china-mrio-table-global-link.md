**Abstract:** This lecture introduces the development of a multi-regional input-output (MRIO) laboratory in China and its linking with main global MRIOs databases for policy-relevant applications. The Chinese Lab features unique flexibility and advances the previous state of the art in terms of three novel aspects. First, it can generate regionally and sectorally very detailed MRIO tables based on users' own research questions. Second, it covers the entire territorial economic boundary and has the longest and most up to date annual time series from 1978 to now. Third, it can be used to provide insight to a wide range of research and policy questions including social, economic, and environmental issues, thus significantly improving all applications that rely on input-output tables. It also introduces a range of technical advances that Chinese Lab are linking with global MRIOs such as GTAP, Eora and Global Lab.

**Biography:** Wang Yafei is a professor and doctoral supervisor at Beijing Normal University, a Distinguished Young Scholar of China's "Ten Thousand Talents Plan", chief expert of a Major Project of the National Social Science Fund of China, and an honoree of the Ministry of Education's Program for New Century Excellent Talents. She holds a PhD in Economics from Dongbei University of Finance and Economics and completed postdoctoral research in Environmental Science and Engineering at Tsinghua University. Her specialization is economic statistics, with research focusing on national accounts. She has published over 30 papers in leading journals such as *Economic Systems Research* and *Statistical Research*. Some of her work has been recognized as ESI Highly Cited Papers and reprinted in full by *Periodical Literatures Reprinted by Renmin University of China*. She has led more than 20 national and provincial-level research projects, including four funded by the National Social Science Fund (a Major Project, a Key Project, a General Project, and a Youth Project). She has won several major research awards, including the National Statistical Research Outstanding Achievement Award and the Beijing Philosophy and Social Sciences Outstanding Achievement Award. She has also contributed extensive policy advisory and think-tank services for institutions including the National Bureau of Statistics, the Ministry of Finance, the Ministry of Education, the National Intellectual Property Administration, and the State Oceanic Administration.

#### 1.4 Issues

![](https://i.loli.net/2020/10/30/gIEWe9wf5qKL3hQ.png)

Compilation Issues:

- Sectors are inconsistent and too few in number.
- Lags in timeliness are substantial; for instance, how can we currently use MRIO to analyze the "dual circulation" model with its emphasis on the domestic cycle?
- There are major structural differences between China's IOAs and international tables.
- Many teams compiling MRIO tables are not engaged in economics research but do so out of application needs; they lack rigor in foundational concepts and theory.

Application Issues:

- De facto tourism encompasses more than just tourism narrowly defined; it also includes food and beverage, transportation, cultural products, etc.
- Sector ≠ Industry!!!
