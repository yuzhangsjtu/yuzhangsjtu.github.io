Abstract: This lecture introduces the development of a multi-regional input-output (MRIO) laboratory in China and its linking with main global MRIOs databases for policy-relevant applications. The Chinese Lab features unique flexibility and advances the previous state of the art in terms of three novel aspects. First, it can generate regionally and sectorally very detailed MRIO tables based on users’ own research questions. Second, it covers the entire territorial economic boundary and has the longest and most up to date annual time series from 1978 to now. Third, it can be used to provide insight to a wide range of research and policy questions including social, economic, and environmental issues, thus significantly improving all applications that rely on input-output tables. It also introduces a range of technical advances that Chinese Lab are linking with global MRIOs such as GTAP, Eora and Global Lab.

Speaker bio: Wang Yafei, female, is a professor and doctoral supervisor at Beijing Normal University, a Young Top-Notch Talent of the National "Ten Thousand Talents Program", chief expert of a Major Project of the National Social Science Fund of China, and a selectee of the Ministry of Education's Program for New Century Excellent Talents. #### 1.4 Problems

![](https://i.loli.net/2020/10/30/gIEWe9wf5qKL3hQ.png)

Compilation problems:

- Sectors are not fixed, and there are too few of them;

- The time lag is far too large; for example, how can MRIO be used right now to analyze dual circulation (双循环)? With domestic circulation as the mainstay?

- There are fairly large structural differences between China's IOA and international tables;

- Many of the teams compiling MRIO tables are not doing economics research; they compile only because of application needs, and are somewhat lacking in basic concepts and theory.

Application problems:

- Tourism in reality includes not only tourism in the narrow sense, but also catering, transportation, cultural products, and so on;
- Sector ≠ industry!!!

Q&A:

Q1: A Peking University graduate now at Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics (Hu Xiurong); she works on CGE and was very excited to see the IOTs. She is currently building a CGE model for China's 30 provinces and needs to extend it to the global level. The current version is nested into GTAP10. What is the price situation for GTAP? Is anyone in China currently using nested data for CGE?

A1: GTAP10 costs roughly 100,000 RMB. CGE requires a SAM; does the fourth quadrant need to be compiled? IOT→SAM→CGE. We have GTAP on hand, but cannot provide it (we do not hold the rights). If you have a GTAP table yourself, you can let us do the linking. In our own workflow, we use **basic prices** for the linking. So far we have not come across CGE being done with nested tables.

Q2: A junior schoolmate of the previous questioner. She is currently using CGE to study transport costs and needs the point-to-point marginal transport cost per unit of product. She has city-level transport distance data for China; how can distance be converted into marginal cost?

A2: Point-to-point transport data are needed. Refer to the 2017-18 national IOTs: extract, then process. Take a look at the handbooks. There may not be a particularly good method.
