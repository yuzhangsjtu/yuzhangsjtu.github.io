My time at Nanjing University accounts for only 3/n of my life, yet the weight of its influence on my future is 3<sup>n</sup>. I once felt the days stretch endlessly before me and graduation seemed so far away — as if the intensely hot summer of 2018, working on a project with Kui and going to Kunshan for fieldwork, were only yesterday. But then, midway through my studies, the pandemic struck. Confinement to physical spaces accelerated time. The thesis, the university’s 520 anniversary celebrations, graduation photos, all these elements keep reminding me: the graduation season is truly here.

There has been so much luck along this academic path, and so very many people and things to be grateful for.

First, I want to thank my supervisor, Professor Zhong Taiyang. After I completed the recommendation-exemption assessment in 2017, it was Professor Zhong who led me through the door. The mood and the scene of cycling from the No.5 canteen to my supervisor’s office that evening I will never forget. Well-read, knowledgeable and gentle in his manner, under Professor Zhong’s guidance I have experienced the most precious teacher-student bond and fellowship among fellow students. In academic terms, Professor Zhong gave me enough research training: I deeply participated in National Science Foundation projects he led, in horizontal projects and international collaborative projects, all of which brought valuable research experience. The rich academic resources Professor Zhong provided kept members of our research group from ever feeling “under-fed”. He also supported me in taking part in academic conferences; in the exchange and collision of ideas, I expanded my scholarly horizons and strengthened my identity as a scholar. Peaches and plums do not speak, yet a path forms beneath them. In everyday activities — from group meetings, email exchanges one after another, to social engagements on the road — my supervisor himself, with both grace and humour, became a model for me. On important holidays like Mid-Autumn Festival or New Year’s Day, Professor Zhong and his wife would often invite everyone from our group to share a meal together, extending a warmth even closer to that of a family. As a “student struggling to graduate”, I must especially thank him for his guidance and support in my academic training. He gave me exactly the right kind of freedom, letting me grow driven by my own interests while pulling me back ashore just as I was about to go astray. This is the kind of serendipity not to be sought and not to be missed, one that will serve me all my life and for which I shall always remain thankful.

I also extend my thanks to the other teachers within our group for the guidance they provided in daily scholarly activities. Professor Huang Xianjin is the senior patriarch of our group and, at the same time, a leading scholar in the field of land management. Centred round Professor Huang’s presence and fixed with land studies firmly at the heart of what we do, everyone labours to amass around “raised plateaus and soaring peaks”. In the big group activities and in academic lectures, time and again, Professor Huang illuminates the bearing of a true Nanjing University scholar with his uniquely puissant insights and erudition – an academic inspiration for me. In counselling, with dissertation advice to developing research proposals for discussion, I must mention and thank Professor Chen Yi, Professor Chen Zhigang, Professor Chuai Xiaowei, and Professor Mao Xiyan for the course-opening support and the like that they provided.
