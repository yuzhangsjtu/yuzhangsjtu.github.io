My time at Nanjing University accounts for only 3/n of my life, yet the magnitude of NJU's influence on my future is 3<sup>n</sup>. I once felt that the years were long and graduation a distant prospect, as if those days in the blazing summer of 2018, working on a project alongside Kui and going to Kunshan for fieldwork, were just yesterday. Yet midway through my studies, the pandemic struck, and the constriction of space "accelerated" time. The thesis, the 520 university anniversary celebration, the graduation photos, and so on -- all these elements were reminding me that the graduation season had truly arrived.

There has been so much luck along my academic path, and so many people and things to be grateful for.

First, I want to thank my supervisor, Professor Zhong Taiyang. After I completed the recommendation-for-admission assessment in 2017, it was Professor Zhong who led me through the door, and I will remember for the rest of my life the mood and the scene of cycling from the No. 5 canteen toward his office that evening. Professor Zhong is learned and erudite, gentle and refined; under his guidance, I gained a precious teacher-student bond and fellowship among schoolmates. In terms of academic leadership, Professor Zhong gave me ample research training. By deeply participating in the Natural Science Foundation projects he led, as well as horizontal projects and international collaborative projects, I gained valuable research experience. The abundant academic resources Professor Zhong provided meant that members of the research group never had to worry about being "underfed". At the same time, he supported me in attending many academic conferences, where, through the exchange and collision of ideas, I broadened my academic horizons and strengthened my sense of identity as a scholar. The peach and plum trees do not speak, yet a path forms beneath them. In daily activities -- from group meetings and letter after letter of correspondence to social occasions on business trips -- my teacher set an example for me through his own refined and humorous image. On important festivals such as the Mid-Autumn Festival and New Year's Day, the professor and his wife would often invite members of the research group to dine together, making me feel a family-like warmth. As someone "struggling to graduate", I must especially thank my teacher for the guidance and help he gave me in academic training. He gave me just the right amount of freedom, allowing me to grow freely, driven by my own interests, while pulling me back ashore just as I was about to go astray. This is a kind of serendipity that cannot be sought but only encountered, one that I will benefit from for the rest of my life and for which I am grateful.

I am grateful to the various teachers within the research group for the guidance they gave me in daily research activities. Professor Huang Xianjin is the patriarch of the group, and at the same time a leading scholar in the field of land management. Everyone gathers around Professor Huang, taking land research as the core to "build plateaus and raise peaks". Through one group activity and academic lecture after another, Professor Huang, with his original insights and erudite image, expounded the temperament of an NJU person, and he is my academic role model. I am grateful to Professor Chen Yi, Professor Chen Zhigang, Professor Chuai Xiaowei, and Professor Mao Xiyan for their help with courses, proposal defenses, and the like. Their research directions vary, yet all take human geography as their core, demonstrating the advantage of viewing and analyzing problems from the comprehensive perspective of the geographical discipline. As a cross-disciplinary student, from the research of these teachers, I came to appreciate the importance of comprehensive thinking.

At the same time, I am grateful to the many close friends I met, who gave me so much support when I was down. Thanks to Rongrong and Brother Hou from the former 520 dorm. Brother Hou would often call to encourage me to look forward and keep moving forward, offering me unconditional encouragement and never reproaching me. Rongrong, the very embodiment of "excellence" -- publishing in C-journals, winning the national scholarship, applying to prestigious schools -- whenever I gazed toward Luojia, I could always sense Rongrong's clever and hardworking figure, which imperceptibly spurred me onward. Thanks to Dr. Wang, who never forgot to discuss scholarship up on Yujia Mountain; through regular literature reports, Dr. Wang helped me broaden my academic horizons and quantitative skills, so that even in a geoscience environment I still learned some economics and management methods. Thanks to senior sister Yan Menglu for helping me apply to enter campus early for the summer, teaching me how to file reimbursements, and helping me find a workstation in the lab. Thanks to senior brother Yuan Ye for his advice on the MRIO method and thesis ideas. Thanks to my schoolmate Yang Kui for the encouragement and companionship he gave me over nearly four years. Brother Kui is an extremely outstanding person, having already resolved his graduation issues back in his senior undergraduate year (when I was still interning in Wuhan). After the semester began, we worked on projects together, attended conferences, and co-authored papers, and Brother Kui gave me, an academic novice, a great deal of guidance. He was never angered by my dullness, always patiently providing answers. In our daily exchanges, we could calmly discuss our views on many issues, conversing on the same wavelength, and I always came away having gained something. Brother Kui is the "second supervisor" of my master's career; I wish him to ride the winds and break the waves in the capital and reap success. Thanks to junior sisters Xie Minqi, Liu Min, Liang Yajia, and Xu Fei, who filled the research group with laughter. Thanks to all the friends in the group, whose hardworking figures kept Room 413 lit up even late into the night.

Finally, the ones I want to thank are my parents. Even as I am about to step into the next stage of life, I still feel that I am very immature, always taking for granted that I can rely on them, and that, as a student, I can still ask them for help. And they, too, always give me unconditional tolerance and support. In the days to come, all I can do is strive forward and live up to their hopes.

Over the past three years, I did many things, yet it seems I accomplished nothing. I tried many things, and I also avoided many things, so that when the graduation season arrived I was caught off guard. I have always struggled between "I ought to love research" and "I have not yet truly fallen in love with learning", thinking too much and doing too little. Fortunately, everything has not yet reached its end, and I still have the chance to strive. The help I received over the past three years will give me greater courage to move forward.

I still firmly believe that one day, I will be able to fall in love with research with a free heart.
