> Peer effect, also known as **peer influence**, refers to the phenomenon where an individual's behaviors, attitudes, or performance are shaped by the peers or group members around them. Peer pressure is a force that easily emerges among peers.
>
> This paper uncovers the adverse impact of competition intensity on peer effects by analyzing randomized dormitory assignment data from a Chinese university, finding that high-ability roommates can negatively affect students’ academic performance in highly competitive environments.

## Essay

Competition, an influential incentive mechanism, is frequently employed to elevate individuals' achievements and performances. Yet, in many domains — education prominently — competition can backfire, because achievement depends not only on personal effort but also on collaboration among peers.

Theoretical studies propose that competition diminishes individuals' willingness to help each other and may even provoke sabotage between rivals. For example, Drago and Garvey (1998) argue that competition undermines colleagues' inclination to assist one another; Lazear (1989) highlights that such contest-based rivalry can trigger acts of sabotage.

These shifts in peer interactions brought by competition prove particularly detrimental within settings that demand cooperation. **Higher education** essentially fits that very bill: here, students typically need to cooperate even as they vie for **high GPAs**, scholarships, and **leadership positions**. Yet, while competition pervades the landscape of higher education, research about how it sculpts peer effects and student exchanges remains woefully scant.

This study aims to fathom how competition sways the academic performance of students, realizing it by estimating and drawing cross-environment comparisons of peer effects under varying degrees of competition. Bolstered by the aleatoric nature of dormitory assignments, this study can supply an indispensable **causal explanation** for peer effects across these gradients of competition.
