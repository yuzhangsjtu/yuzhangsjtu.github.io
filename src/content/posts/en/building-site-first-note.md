When the temperature falls by more than 10°C within a day and a night, and the minimum temperature drops below 5°C, that is a cold wave.

So the sudden plunge in temperature over these two days can be called a cold wave; it counts, I suppose, as taking the definition from high school geography class out for another review.

This cold air is said to come from Siberia, that faraway northern land, where a strong cold high-pressure system still formed even as the subsolar point stepped across the equator and headed north. My window faces north, so I met it head-on. After being lectured and laughed at twice by my mom, my stubbornness finally proved no match for the spring cold snap, and I obediently put on my long johns.

Today I talked over some things with a friend: the same tangled family disputes, which have dragged on for twenty or thirty years, with no idea how they could ever be brought to a close. In the past, no matter the circumstances, I would never have imagined home in the shape of a burden, yet now the thought occasionally surfaces. I want to abandon it; I want to escape. I feel myself gradually becoming worldly.

Today is also my dad's birthday, the fifth day of the third lunar month, which I had been keeping in mind for a long time. In years past I was always at school around this time and would mail him a few small gifts; this year, stuck at home with no other choice, I wasn't sure what to prepare and figured the simpler the better. In the afternoon I went to the most delightful pastry shop in this small town and bought a little cake. With a bit of selfishness, I also bought myself a mille-crepe while I was at it, a new strawberry flavor I had never tried. At this shop I have had the matcha mille-crepe and the mango mille-crepe before; wonderful flavors. My dad is an odd character and did not appreciate the gesture at all. He said he absolutely refuses to eat dessert... So the little cake could only lie alone in the fridge; I shared the mille-crepe with my mom and went back to my room, sulking. He said that as long as I still remember his birthday, that is enough, and there is no need for any other ritual.

Once again I spent the whole day fiddling with the Hexo site, and I switched to the Fluid theme. I have to say, this theme's features and usability are better than the previous one's, and the material design styling looks great too; after some simple personalization and configuration it is ready to use. There should be more custom tailoring later on. Also, this theme's background image seems only so-so at adapting to mobile devices, so some optimization will be needed down the road.

Having stayed at home for so long, I seem to carry more and more hostility in me: a kind of impotent rage at wanting to become a gentle person yet always failing. Perhaps, without realizing it, I have been colored by the oppressive atmosphere of the family. Many times I failed to keep my temper in check, took a bad attitude when speaking to my mom, and said harsh words, only to regret them the very next second, over and over again. I should still learn to talk with myself more, to let myself slow down, slow down, and think things through.

The whole winter break has been a blur of confusion. The plans I made before leaving school are still just a list sitting in my head; I always lack the drive to put them into practice. My paper went through rejection, resubmission, and rejection again, the other papers on my hands have made no progress either, and I keep resisting writing. I love school very much, but I do not love studying, and that is a fatal thing. Courage really is not an abstract word.

Legend has it that in faraway Central Asia there are vast fields of sunflowers. That was one of the names she once gave me. To live facing the sun and to grow upward: that is what I long to be.

As ever, I make my bedtime wish: tomorrow I must settle down and write my paper, for research is my everything.
