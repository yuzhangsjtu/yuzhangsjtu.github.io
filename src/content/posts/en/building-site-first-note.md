When the temperature drops by more than 10 ℃ within a full day, and the minimum dips below 5 ℃, it qualifies as a cold wave.

So this sharp plummet in temperature over the last couple of days does amount to a cold wave. Kind of feels like pulling an old definition from high-school geography class off the shelf and revising it again.

They say this batch of frigid air hails from Siberia — that far-off northern land, where powerful cold high-pressure systems still breed even as the direct sunrays stride past the equator and head north. My window faces north, which means I took the blast head on. After two lessons of my mum lecturing and ribbing me sharp enough even children could grasp the principle, my stubbornness still folded in the face of a deep spring chill enough to warn “spring ox still fears cold ash”, and I dutifully pulled on my thermal trousers.

Caught up with a friend on some matters earlier today — the same tangled-up family quarrels, running on for twenty, thirty years without any visible off-ramp to a rest. Before this, never once, in any situation, had I thought of family shaped like a burden; now, occasionally, the thought surfaces — a wish just to discard, to run. I feel myself, bit by bit, growing mediocre.

Today is also my dad’s birthday, the one I keep careful track of — the fifth of the third lunar month. Most years at this time I’d still be at university, sending little gifts his way. This year I was stuck at home unavoidably, uncertain what to prepare, mulling over how the more straightforward the better. In the afternoon I went to the most swelling-with-contentment pastry shop in this little town and bought a small cake. Self-interest nudged in: I also bought myself a mille-crepe — a new strawberry-flavoured item I hadn’t yet tried. Before this at that shop, I’d had matcha and mango mille-crepes, exquisite flavours. My dad turned out to be a complete one-off with no room for bending comprehension; he said he absolutely does not eat desserts… So the tiny cake could only lie forlorn in the fridge. Shared the mille-crepe with my mum, then withdrew to my room, deflated. He commented, it really was enough just to know I remembered the day — no other marker was worth stamping a footnote upon.

Another whole day scrambling away with setting up a static blog on Hexo: swapped in the “Fluid” theme. I have to hand it to this theme — its functionality and usability feel a big notch above my old ones; the material-design look is handsome, too. Simple customising is done, out-of-box it goes alright; later there’ll likely be deeper tailored tweaks. Face-notes, though: this theme’s hero images do only so-so with a mobile screen; optimization at some later point is justified.

Staying home for this long seems to pile savage edges inside myself higher by the day. There is this impulse — this fail-small, incompetent-furious howl-to-the-sky that happens every time I want so much to be a soft, gentle‑spirited person. Maybe, without my own volition agreeing, the suppressive atmosphere at home has somehow stained me thoroughly. A great many times I failed to harness my temper, spoke true chafed notes talking to my mum — said brash harrying-ammunition words, then the merest breathed moment later was flooded utterly with regret. On it rolls. The thing for me having left is still to find yet more communing with myself — let myself slow down, slow down; give a lot more space for considering.
