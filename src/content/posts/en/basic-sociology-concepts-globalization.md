## Globalization

> The various processes that bring geographically dispersed populations closer, increase communication, and make the entire planet a community of fate or a global society.

### 1 Origins and History

The idea of building a global human community can be traced back to eighteenth-century Enlightenment period discussions on whether “humanity as a whole” was possible. In the nineteenth century, such discussions gradually increased, such as Marx’s insight into the global expansion of capitalism, Durkheim’s view on the division of labor transcending geographic boundaries, and so on. Yet it was not until 1961 that “globalization” in the modern sense first appeared in a dictionary-type publication, and economists did not begin using the term frequently until the late 1980s.

A well-known starting point for early sociological engagement with globalization is Wallerstein’s world-systems theory. According to Wallerstein, the capitalist economic system transcends national borders and constitutes a world-system, with relatively wealthy countries forming the core, impoverished countries the periphery, and semi-peripheral countries lying between the two. Deeper discussions of globalization were driven by the process accelerating in the 1970s: at the time, multinational corporations rapidly expanded and grew in power, many began to worry about the decline of the nation-state, supranational trade blocs and regional politico-economic entities (such as the EU) emerged, transport developed and improved, making cross-border tourism and migration increasingly common, and the advent of the internet made instant international communication possible. By the 1990s, the concept of globalization had entered mainstream sociological discourse, exerting a profound influence on almost every subfield of the discipline.

> Thirty years later, under the grip of the COVID-19 epidemic, the broader trend of globalization has suffered setbacks and in fact has already begun to reverse.

### 2 Meaning and Interpretation

Where globalization comes from and how it affects us — opinions vary and there is no consensus. Globalization reminds us that this is a process of change, or a social tide, in which different corners of the world become interdependent and more closely connected. But that does not guarantee that a unified global society will necessarily come into being. Globalization has multiple dimensions, including the economic, political, and cultural. For some, globalization is primarily an economic dimension, encompassing the globalization of financial transactions, trade, production and consumption, a global division of labor, and a world financial system. Economic globalization accelerates population movement and brings changes to transport and habitation, making human ways of life ever more mobile. Some hold that cultural globalization is even more important. To capture how local communities actively reshape the process in order to adapt it to their distinctive local cultures, Robertson (1995) coined the concept of **glocalization**, the fusion of global characteristics and local elements. From this perspective, globalization is the multidirectional flow of cultural products around the world. Lastly, yet another group concerns itself with political globalization, attending above all to the establishment and development of regional and international mechanisms of inter-governmental cooperation, such as the United Nations and the European Union. Such institutions bring nation-states and international NGOs together on a common, if not shared, decision-making platform, coordinating this rapidly developing globalized process.

Accelerated globalization is generally dated to the 1970s. This rapid globalization process has profoundly influenced our lives. A decision in one corner of the world can have a huge impact in some faraway corner, and the nation-state, the most important actor on the world stage to date, appears weary and faded, its former prestige, power, and powers of control ebbing away.

### 3 Criticism and Discussion

Globalization theorists believe that the globalization process dramatically transforms the way people live, but some scholars remain skeptical, contending that this view greatly exaggerates such effects. Scholars critical of globalization (also known as skeptics) argue that, while cross-border interactions and connectivity today are indeed far greater than in the past, a unified world market system has not yet emerged. On the contrary, the vast majority of trade is still concentrated within a few regional zones, such as the EU area, the Asia-Pacific region, and North America. These three regional economic blocs operate fairly independently, leading skeptics to assert that the notion of a truly global economic system remains far too premature.

Likewise, the idea that globalization has diminished the role of the nation-state has also been challenged to varying degrees. The reality shows us that national governments still play indispensable roles in supervising and coordinating economic activity, signing trade deals, and laying down economic liberalization policies. Alliances and cooperation between sovereign states do not inevitably weaken them. However tightly integrated the international system becomes, nation-states retain substantial state power and adopt an increasingly proactive and open-minded attitude toward the rapidly globalizing external environment. So globalization is not a one-way street toward ever tighter integration, but a dual dynamic, bidirectional, interacting current that may give rise to many different outcomes.

> Viewed from 2021, the above criticisms may matter more than ever. Among countries that share the same cultural background and social institutions, globalization may indeed dissolve boundaries. But in nations whose ideology, institutional fabric, and cultural backgrounds are very different, nationalist sentiment surges just as these countries profit from the globalization process — what we witness is in effect global contestation, or the rewriting of the traditional dominant global discourses.
