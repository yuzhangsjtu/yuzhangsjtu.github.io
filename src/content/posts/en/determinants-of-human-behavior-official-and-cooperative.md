The common thread between the two papers introduced in this issue is that both focus on the determinants of human behavior, both were published in the Economic Journal (EJ), and both tell China stories.

The first paper examines how officials' early-life experiences of famine influence the fiscal expenditure allocations they make when serving as the top leader of a county. The second, based on a field experiment, shows that the origins of cooperative behavior are tied to rice farming—extending the Science paper on rice and wheat. The primary method used in the first paper is staggered-cohort DiD, and the authors have made the full code available, making it a good starting point for beginners to get hands-on practice.
