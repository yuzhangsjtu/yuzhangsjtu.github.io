What the two papers introduced in this issue have in common is that they both focus on the determinants of human behavior, both were published in EJ, and both are China stories.

The first paper examines how officials' early famine experiences affect their fiscal expenditure bias when they serve as the top leader of a district or county. The second, based on a field experiment, shows that the origins of cooperative behavior are related to rice farming, and is a continuation of the Science paper on rice and wheat. The main method of the first paper is cohort DID, and the paper also provides all of its code, which beginners can use to learn hands-on.
