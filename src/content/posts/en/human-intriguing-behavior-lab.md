“Human Intriguing Behavior Laboratory” (**HIBL**) — I spent a long time mulling over the name.

Runners-up included “Human Intriguing Behavior Observation Station,” “Intriguing Human Behavior Lab,” or, in Douban style, “Observational Group on Intriguing Human Conduct.” Tossing it around on sleepless nights, I kept coming back to “Human Intriguing Behavior Laboratory,” because a “**Lab**” usually does more than observe — it also involves **creating**.

I’m drawn to intriguing human behaviors and want to understand how they are expressed, how they’re discovered, how they are interpreted, and what deeper, more structural determinants lie behind them. Given my academic background, I focus mainly on how the social sciences and the natural sciences that stand close to them make sense of intriguing human behaviors — including explanations rooted in **geographic environment**, **historical institutional pathways**, chance events, and **economics**, particularly those parts that can be considered causal.

Through wide reading, observation and reflection, I aim to share facts and mechanisms about intriguing human behavior — for example, **the origins of institutions**, **geographic variation in human behavior** — and I also strive, in the course of disseminating and exchanging ideas, to discover more intriguing human behaviors and their interpretations. The literature I draw on comes mainly from economics (especially the field of quantitative history), political science, geography journals and general-interest periodicals; physiological and psychological sources are by and large not included.

As an individual human being, I see this as a thrilling and fascinating journey — a journey that’s about coming to know the world, and at the same time turning inward to search within myself.
