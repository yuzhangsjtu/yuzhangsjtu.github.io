"Human Intriguing Behavior Laboratory" (**HIBL**) — I spent a long time mulling over this name.

Other candidates included "Human Intriguing Behavior Observation Station," "Intriguing Human Behavior Laboratory," or, in Douban style, "Observation Group for Intriguing Human Conduct." Turning it over on sleepless nights, I still felt "Human Intriguing Behavior Laboratory" was the better choice, because a "**Lab**" usually does more than just observe — it also involves **creating**.

I'm interested in intriguing human behaviors. I want to know how intriguing behaviors are expressed and discovered, how they are interpreted, and what more structural determining factors lie behind them. Because of my academic background, I focus mainly on how the social sciences and the natural sciences close to them understand intriguing human behaviors — including explanations of human behavior drawn from the **geographic environment**, **historical institutional pathways**, chance events, and **economics**, particularly those parts of such explanations that can be regarded as causal.

Through wide reading of the literature, observation, and reflection, I will disseminate facts and mechanisms about intriguing human behavior — such as **the origins of institutions** and **the geographic variation of human behavior** — and I am also committed to discovering and interpreting more intriguing human behaviors in the course of dissemination and exchange. This literature comes mainly from journals in economics (especially the field of quantitative history), political science, and geography, as well as general-interest periodicals, but generally does not include physiological and psychological materials.

As an individual human being, I believe this is a thrilling and intriguing journey, because this journey is about coming to know the world and at the same time turning inward to seek out myself.
