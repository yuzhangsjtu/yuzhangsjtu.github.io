There are some people best kept at arm's length.

April Fools' Day. When young people and students are dispersed, each adrift on their own, the festive mood of days like this seems to dissolve into nothing. Just another ordinary day.

On WeChat Moments, only a handful of posts marking the anniversary of Leslie Cheung's passing — a ritual carried out as if by routine. Bilibili has an April Fools' event going on, but honestly, it barely registers.

And yet, here we are, stepping into an April steeped in falsehoods.

All day today, I've been organizing data with dreadful efficiency — pulling numbers from yearbooks, double-checking figures, doing work I've already done before, just earmarking revisions. Meanwhile, another task of mine hasn't even been touched yet: a great deal of data cleaning and format conversion. I still can't figure out why the advisor wants SPSS format — isn't Stata perfectly fine?

By nightfall, I was still wrestling with the data when some mystery man added me on WeChat. Claimed to be a third-year master's student from my own department, soon to pursue a PhD at PKU. His clearly designated field of acceptance was Human Geography, yet he insisted he's in Regional Economics. Hush-hush about who his advisor is here, and secretive about which department he'll join at PKU. Still, he felt obligated to opine that my research group tends toward insularity and suggested I go out more often — you know, "explore." Reached out to a senior peer of mine and got a WeChat share in return: the "Taj Mahal is located in Thailand" hot take, a classic. He helped me interpret the previous gentleman — now confirmed, a basket case, the sort better watched from far afield.

In barely two more weeks, I'll reach the 3-month mark since "winter" holiday began. The days drift by, time squandered — old regrets, new follies worn smooth.

Treasure time, live for learning — from this foolish April — can someone tell if I'm deceiving even myself?
