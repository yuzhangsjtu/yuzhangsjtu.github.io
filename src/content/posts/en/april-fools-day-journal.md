There are some people you need to keep at arm's length.

April Fools' Day. If the young people and students are scattered apart, each adrift on their own, the atmosphere of this holiday seems to vanish into nothing. This is an ordinary day.

On WeChat Moments there are only a scant few posts commemorating Leslie Cheung, which seems like a routine ritual. Bilibili has an April Fools' event, but it honestly barely registers.

And yet we have still entered an April full of lies.

I spent the whole day today inefficiently organizing data: digging through yearbooks, cross-checking figures, repeating work I had already done, and checking for updates. I have another task that I haven't even started, a fairly large batch of data cleaning and format conversion. I don't understand why the advisor wants it in SPSS format. Isn't Stata perfectly good?

In the evening, while still organizing data, I was added on WeChat by a strange man. He claimed to be a third-year master's student in our school, about to go to PKU for a PhD. Although his admitted field was plainly human geography, he insisted he was in regional economics. His advisor at our school was confidential, and so was where he was headed at PKU. He also said that our research group is rather closed off and suggested I go out and see more... I talked it over with the senior fellow student in the year above me, and he sent me a WeChat Moments post about "the Taj Mahal is in Thailand," leaving me to work it out for myself. Confirmed: a prize oddball, to be kept at arm's length.

In less than two weeks, it will be three months since winter break started. I continue dawdling and regretting, foolishly wasting away the days.

Cherish your time, study hard: will those be the lies I tell myself in this April of fools?

I always spend my time on these mundane things.

I still haven't fallen in love with studying.
