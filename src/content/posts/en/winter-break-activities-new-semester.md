##### 1.2 Term Paper for *International Relations and Global Governance*

《Discourse Analysis of Chinese Public Opinion on the Russia-Ukraine Conflict — Based on Official Media and Weibo Data》

**Main Tasks:**

- To analyze the characteristics, trends, and generative mechanisms of Chinese public opinion regarding the Russia-Ukraine conflict.
- Method: discourse analysis.
- Data:
  - Reports from Chinese official entities (collected sporadically with focuses)
  - Sina Weibo's daily trending data: from January 1, 2022 to December 31, 2022, acquired hourly.
  - A dataset of Sina Weibo user discussions about the conflict, spanning from February 24, 2022 to December 31, 2022, totaling 51,711 entries.
- Findings:
  - Compared with Europe, America, and other regions, Chinese official labeling of the Russia-Ukraine conflict carries a neutral character.
  - Weibo user posts and opinions can be classified into seven types: supporting Ukraine, supporting Russia, peace and anti-war, mockery, engaging in meme culture, watching from the sidelines and complaining, and the "America manipulation theory".
  - Pro-Ukraine and pro-Russia viewpoints demonstrate conflict polarization. Their generative mechanisms are related to the collective memory formed during China's state-building and the realities of present-day conflicts.
