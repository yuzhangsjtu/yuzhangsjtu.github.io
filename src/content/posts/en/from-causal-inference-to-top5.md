#### 1 Textbooks

-  [**Causal Inference** *The Mixtape*](https://mixtape.scunning.com/) 

  My personal favorite, and it even has a web version! It strikes a good balance between detail and brevity, and in the preface the author explains his original motivation for writing it: he lacked a textbook that combined both theory and empirics (reproducibility). This book provides a large number of classic paper cases, paired with Stata, R and Python code — you could call it a hand-holding, nanny-style textbook!

- [ **Causal Inference**: What If](https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/miguel-hernan/causal-inference-book/) 

  This book is also excellent. Compared with the previous one, it seems a bit more professional and demanding. The author likewise provides accompanying data and code; the pity is that this book is only available as a PDF, with no web version. In addition, this book has a fan-made [Chinese translation](https://jiajunluo.mystrikingly.com/), and while searching today I found that someone seems to have also made a [document](https://causalai.github.io/what-if/).

- [**The Effect**: An Introduction to Research Design and Causality](https://theeffectbook.net/)

  Compared with the previous two, the biggest difference of this book is "almost no math." The author tries, using nothing but plain text and causal diagrams, to tell readers the key points of causal inference. At the same time, this book also has a web version, and provides data and code in multiple languages!

- *Basically Useful Econometrics* and *Advanced Econometrics and Stata Applications*. Surely everyone already knows these two. I feel both books are quite good and suitable for browsing. The pity is that the accompanying cases in both books are too small or too few.

</br>

#### 2 Research Tools

The mainstream econometric software is nothing more than Stata, R and Python; some friends who do equilibrium models and structural estimation may also use Matlab. My sense is that in the GPT era, the barrier to getting started with code may be the smallest obstacle in learning econometrics.

There are some materials that may be quite helpful for mastering the basic syntax of these languages. They are:

- [Python-Fundamentals](https://github.com/dlab-berkeley/Python-Fundamentals)
- [R-Fundamentals](https://github.com/dlab-berkeley/R-Fundamentals)
- [Stata-Fundamentals](https://github.com/dlab-berkeley/Stata-Fundamentals)

⬆️ These are all repos from Berkeley's D-Lab. The content is all beginner-oriented and especially suitable for getting started; the author makes the beginner tutorials in the form of notebooks, which is really thoughtful.

</br>

#### 4 Other Possibly Useful Materials

- [Bilibili](https://www.bilibili.com/) University — I learned Professor Chen Qiang's introductory econometrics basically all on Bilibili. Here I also recommend an up-loader, **[silencedream](https://space.bilibili.com/9840842)**, who explains several causal inference methods fairly clearly at the conceptual level.
